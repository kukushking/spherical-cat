You Can't Comb the Hair on a Spherical Cat

The Hairy Ball Theorem states that any continuous tangent vector field on a sphere must have at least one point where the vector is zero. In other words, if a sphere is covered in hair, you can never comb it all flat without creating at least one cowlick or bald spot. Try the different fields below to see why!
